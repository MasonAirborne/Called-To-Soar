About Me

Mason Airborne —
After years of uncertainty and dodging the classic holiday questions, I finally have an answer I can say with confidence: I'm becoming a pilot.

My path to aviation wasn’t linear, but in many ways, it was building in the background all along. At 26, I made the commitment. Not a leap—but a decision born of grit, sacrifice, and years of quiet perseverance. On July 16th, 2025, I completed my first solo flight with 27 hours logged. That’s when the journey to SOAR truly began.

Today, I’m immersed in the corporate aviation world, assisting aircraft in extreme weather, maintaining ramp safety, and gaining exposure to what keeps the industry moving. When I watch pilots—especially women—step off a Pilatus PC-24, a King Air, or even a Gulfstream, it reignites my purpose. I want to be one of them.

This is my invitation to you: stop asking “Why me?”
Start declaring—“Why not me?”
