About Me

For years, I dreaded the classic questions:

“So… what do you want to be when you grow up?”
“What are you doing these days?”

Now, I finally have an answer I can say with absolute confidence:
I’m becoming a pilot.

How I Got Here

My path to aviation wasn’t straight. I didn’t set foot in a cockpit until I was 22. But the seed had been planted long before that—and at 26, I finally made the decision.

Not a leap. A decision. Born out of grit, sacrifice, and years of quiet perseverance. On July 16, 2025, with 27 hours logged, I completed my first solo flight. That’s when the journey to SOAR truly began.

Resilience Built on the Ground

As a first-generation college graduate, I earned my business degree in 2021 debt-free.

  • 25+ scholarships
  • Summers packed with classes
  • Countless late nights in the library

That season taught me discipline, resilience, and the value of showing up—even when the odds aren’t in your favor.

The Pivot Point

After years of draining jobs, I decided to start where I was with what I had. I got a job at my local Class Charlie airport. Today, I’m in the thick of corporate aviation: fueling, marshaling, battling extreme weather, and learning how the industry moves.

Every time I watch a pilot—especially a woman—step off a Pilatus PC-24, King Air, or Gulfstream, it reignites my purpose: I will be one of them.

What I’ve Learned

I’ve had my share of doubts. “Why didn’t I start sooner?” or "Am I good enough?" But I’ve learned that every year of waiting was building something in me. Every delay fortified my character. My journey wasn’t late—it was right on time.

Why I Share This

Aviation isn’t just a dream to me. It’s a responsibility. It demands safety, judgment, and professionalism. And this is my invitation to you:

Stop asking, “Why me?”
Start declaring, “Why not me?”
