About Me

I never imagined that all those childhood flights across state lines would lead me here—chasing the skies not as a passenger, but as a pilot. My path to aviation didn’t start in a straight line, but in many ways, it was preparing me all along. I didn’t sit in the cockpit until I was 22, but the seed had been planted long before. After years of exploring different paths, I finally committed myself to flying at 26. It wasn’t a sudden leap, but a decision shaped by years of perseverance, sacrifice, and growth.

In 2021, I graduated from my dream school—the University of Tennessee—with a degree in Business and a minor in Marketing. I was a first-generation college student who transferred from a community college where I played collegiate basketball. My team, a true Cinderella story, went all the way to the Tennessee state championship and lost in a grueling triple-overtime game. That loss taught me resilience. I went on to complete my degree debt-free, after working tirelessly, applying for scholarships, attending summer school, and spending nights in the library until midnight. I learned the value of showing up consistently—even when the odds were stacked against me.

After saving over $20,000 for flight school and another $30,000 for retirement, I quit a stable but unfulfilling job to pursue flying full-time. I now have 20 hours of flight time, study four to five hours daily, and fly two hours a day. Recently, I became a part-time airside assistant at my local Class C airport to immerse myself in the operational side of aviation. Whether it’s assisting aircraft in extreme weather or helping maintain ramp safety, I take pride in doing the "grunt work" that keeps aviation moving.

Still, there are days I battle self-doubt. I ask myself, “Why didn’t I do this sooner?” But then I remind myself—every year of preparation mattered. Every struggle gave me grit. My journey wasn’t delayed; it was fortified. I’ve learned to give myself grace. I am not behind—I am right on time.

Now, at 27, I see a bigger purpose beyond my own dream. I mentor a young woman who wants to become a pilot. Sharing my experiences, the lessons I wish I knew, and seeing her eyes light up reminds me that this journey is not just about me. It’s about breaking barriers, shattering ceilings, and showing the next generation what’s possible.

Faith Journey

My faith journey has been filled with ups and downs. I always knew Jesus, but my first true encounter with Him came after my parents' divorce. Even though I drifted in my early adult years—partying, drinking, and smoking—He met me again in my despair in 2022. It was then I heard His voice say, “Look up child, you are forgiven. Follow Me and you will be saved.”

That moment felt like a breath of fresh air. Since then, my walk with God has been a daily choice. It’s not always easy—but it’s always worth it. I choose to follow my Savior, to love people radically, to be kind, and to stay joyful no matter what challenges I face. Every day I choose to walk in light, purpose, and grace.
