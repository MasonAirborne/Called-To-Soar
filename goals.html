My Flight Training Goals

My dream is to become a professional corporate pilot — flying King Airs, Pilatus PC-24s, and Gulfstreams across the country and globe. But dreams like this require more than passion — they require funding, commitment, and faith.

I’ve already saved over $20,000 toward training and another $30,000 toward my future. I’ve completed my first solo flight and work part-time at a busy Class C airport to stay immersed in the industry. But advanced ratings like my Instrument, Commercial, and Multi-Engine cost tens of thousands more.

My total financial goal for flight training is approximately $60,000.

I’m actively applying to multiple aviation scholarships including:

  • Women in Aviation International (WAI)
  • Women in Corporate Aviation
  • The Part-Time Pilot Flight Scholarship

Each scholarship gets me one step closer to fulfilling my mission: becoming a CFI, flying corporate, and mentoring the next generation of underrepresented aviators. Your support — whether through sharing my story or contributing directly — helps me stay in the sky and on purpose.

Together, we’re proving that hard work and faith can take you anywhere — even 36,000 feet in the air.
