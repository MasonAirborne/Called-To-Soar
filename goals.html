My Flight Training Goals

My dream is to become a professional corporate pilot — flying jets like the Gulfstream, Bombardier, or Dassault across the skies. It’s not just about the aircraft — it’s about reaching people, building a legacy, and breaking barriers in aviation.

I'm currently working full-time at a busy Class C airport, gaining hands-on experience in operations and fueling my passion for aviation daily. I’ve already saved over $20,000 toward flight school and recently completed my first solo flight — a milestone that marked the beginning of my path to the skies.

But becoming a pilot requires more than passion — it takes funding, focus, and perseverance. The cost of my remaining ratings (Instrument, Commercial, Multi-Engine) adds up quickly. My overall fundraising goal is approximately $60,000.

I'm actively applying for aviation scholarships, including:

  • Women in Aviation International (WAI)
  • Women in Corporate Aviation
  • The Part-Time Pilot Flight Scholarship

Each application, each flight hour, each shift at the airport brings me closer to a future where I can inspire others to follow their dreams — especially young women and underrepresented aspiring aviators.
